Cryptography in the Old Testament



In the old testament a traditional substitution of letters is used, called atbash, in which the last letter of the Hebrew alphabet is replaced by the first, the penultimate by the second and so on, this is a particular example of a substitution cipher, cipher that we will see later. The technique can be observed in the Cabala, in the Dead Sea manuscripts and even in the Old Testament in Jeremiah 25; 26, 51; 41. `` And the king of Sesach (Sheshach) or Babylon (babel) will drink him after them ", replacing SH by b and L by ch.
