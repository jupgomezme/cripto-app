New Applications

  • Molecular computing was introduced by Leonard Adleman in 1994. It is a multidisciplinary effort of Mathematicians, Biochemists and Biologists, who use DNA to code NP problems and produce their solutions (the traveling agent problem). The algorithms involved use enzymes such as polymerase or ligase. One of the applications found for this type of calculation consisted in determining the key space of a DES cryptographic system.
  • The RSA system is based on the impossibility of finding an efficient algorithm to factor large numbers. This problem has been one of the greatest paradigms of science and its solution will produce a remarkable technological advance for humanity. For example, computer security based on RSA will have to be completely changed. Especially the generation of digital signatures.
  • The idea of quantum computing was introduced by Richard Feynman in 1982. A quantum computer uses qubits in a controlled initial state that represent a problem and manipulates them through logic gates. The calculation ends once the measurement of all states collapses each qubit to one of two pure states. The problem of factoring large numbers is efficiently solved in a quantum computer.
  • Post-quantum cryptography originates with the introduction of Shor's algorithm, which solves the integer factorization problem using a quantum computer. At present, lattice-based cryptography and the solution of systems of multivariate equations is considered post-quantum. In it, difficult-to-solve problems associated with these structures allow the construction and analysis of public-key cryptographic systems.
