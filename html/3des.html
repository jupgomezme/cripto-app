Explanation

The 3-DES (Triple Data Encryption Standard) algorithm is based on the DES algorithm. 3-DES is the algorithm that does triple encryption of DES; it is based on applying it three consecutive times, with three different keys, making it much more secure. DES encrypts a 64-bit block and a key of the same length, but as we have seen, in practice, the key only provides 56 bits of security. 3-DES was developed as a more secure alternative due to the small key length of DES. In 3-DES, the DES algorithm is executed three times with three keys, however, it is only considered secure if three separate keys are used, which gives 128 bits of security in the key. More information about the history and operation of the 3-DES cipher can be found on this page in English https://dementium2.com/seguridad-de-informacion/que-es-el-cifrado-3des-y-como-funciona-des/ and interesting information about the cipher in English in the book Computational Science and Its Applications - ICCSA 2019 19th International Conference, Saint Petersburg, Russia, July 1-4, 2019, Proceedings, Part VI: 19th International Conference, Saint Petersburg, Russia, July 1-4, 2019, Proceedings, Part VI.
