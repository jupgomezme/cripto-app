ek(x) = xk           dk(y) = yk−1

When the key is not given as a matrix but as a word, what is done is that the word must have m2 letters for an m ∈ ℕ, in that case we assemble the matrix with rows of m letters and then transform the letters to their number equivalents in ℤ26, for the keyword “aborigins" the key matrix is: $$\begin{bmatrix} a & b & o\\ r & i & g\\ i & n & s \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 14\\ 17 & 8 & 6\\ 8 & 13 & 18 \end{bmatrix}$$
