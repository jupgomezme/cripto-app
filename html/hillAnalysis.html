How does it work?

Let’s analyze the text C F W F B B B A T I Z V T which decrypted is C R Y P T O G R A P H Y assuming m = 2 since it is the first order of a matrix used for Hill’s cipher. First we transform it to numbers to obtain 3 5 22 5 1 1 0 19 8 25 25 21 19. If we separate the text into blocks, from the second and third blocks we have ek(19,14) = (1,1), ek(24,15) = (22,5) and thus obtain the matrix equation

\[\begin{bmatrix} 22 & 5\\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}= \begin{bmatrix} 24 & 15\\ 19 & 14 \end{bmatrix} K\]

where we can calculate without difficulty but with great care with the equivalences modulo 26

\[\begin{split}K= \begin{bmatrix} 19 & 4\\ 23 & 19 \end{bmatrix} \end{split}\]
