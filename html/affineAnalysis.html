How does it work?

When the text is very long we assume that the text is in a known language where there are more or less frequent characters, in the case of English we have that the probabilities of occurrence of a letter in a text from the most probable to the least probable are as follows:

  • E with a probability of 0.120

  • T, A, O, I, N, S, H and R in that order with probabilities between 0.06 y 0.09

  • D and L with probabilities of 0.04 each one

  • C, U, M, W, F, G, Y, P y B with probabilities between 0.015 and 0.028

  • V, K, J, X, Q y Z with probabilities less than 0.01

We also analyzed how in previous exercises the frequency of digraphs such as TH or trigrams such as ING, which are quite frequent in English.
